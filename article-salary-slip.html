Salary & Tax

How to read your first salary slip — PF, TDS, HRA explained simply

Your offer letter said ₹6 LPA. Your bank got ₹38,000. Here's exactly where ₹12,000 went every month — and how to legally keep more of it.

By RupeeSmart Team · 8 min read · Updated June 2025

You got your first job. Your CTC (Cost to Company) is ₹6,00,000 per year. You do the math — that's ₹50,000 a month. Then your salary slip arrives and shows ₹38,200 credited to your account.

Where did ₹11,800 go? This guide explains every single line on your salary slip — and more importantly, which deductions you can reduce legally.

Part 1: Earnings (the additions)

Your salary slip has two halves: earnings (money coming to you) and deductions (money going away). Let's start with earnings.

Basic Salary

This is the foundation. Usually 40–50% of your CTC. Almost all other components — PF, gratuity, HRA — are calculated as a percentage of your basic salary. Higher basic = higher PF deduction but also higher retirement corpus.

HRA (House Rent Allowance)

Typically 40–50% of basic salary (50% in metro cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru). The best part: HRA is partially or fully tax-exempt if you pay rent. If you live in a rented house, you can save a lot here.

💡 HRA Tax Exemption Rule: The exempt amount is the lowest of: (1) actual HRA received, (2) actual rent paid minus 10% of basic, or (3) 50% of basic (metro) / 40% of basic (non-metro). Submit rent receipts to your HR to claim this.

Special Allowance

This is basically "whatever's left after all other components." Fully taxable. No exemption. Companies use this to make your CTC look bigger without giving you real benefits.

LTA (Leave Travel Allowance)

Given for travel during leaves. Tax-exempt twice in a block of 4 years — but only if you actually travel and submit proofs. Many people forget to claim this.

Part 2: Deductions (the subtractions)

PF — Provident Fund (EPF)

12% of your basic salary is deducted every month and goes into your EPF account. Your employer also contributes 12% of basic — but 8.33% of that goes to EPS (pension) and only 3.67% actually goes to your EPF account.

Current EPF interest rate: 8.25% per annum (2024-25) — better than most FDs, plus tax-free on maturity.

TDS — Tax Deducted at Source

Your employer estimates your annual tax liability and deducts a monthly portion. If you declare all your exemptions (HRA, 80C investments, etc.) to HR at the start of the year, your TDS will be lower.

⚠️ Important: If you don't submit investment proofs by December–January, your employer will deduct heavy TDS in the last few months to make up the shortfall. Always submit Form 12BB with your proofs on time.

Professional Tax

A small state-level tax. Maximum ₹2,500 per year. Not applicable in all states — no professional tax in Delhi, for example.

Part 3: Net Take-Home Salary

Gross Salary (₹47,250) − Total Deductions (₹9,050) = ₹38,200 in-hand. That's where your "missing" money went.

How to legally increase your take-home pay

1. Claim HRA exemption if you pay rent

Submit rent receipts and your landlord's PAN (if rent > ₹1 lakh/year) to HR. This alone can save ₹2,000–₹6,000/month in TDS.

2. Max out 80C investments (₹1.5 lakh limit)

Your EPF contribution already counts. Top up with ELSS mutual funds, PPF, or NPS to reach the ₹1.5 lakh limit. Reduces your taxable income and thus TDS.

3. Claim Leave Travel Allowance

Actually travel and submit bills. Two exemptions allowed per 4-year block. Often overlooked and left on the table.

4. Food coupons / meal card

Many companies offer Sodexo or meal card — up to ₹26,400/year is tax-exempt. If your company offers it, opt in.

Bottom line: With proper declarations (HRA + 80C + LTA), someone on ₹6 LPA in a metro can reduce their TDS from ₹4,800/month to nearly ₹0 — legally and completely within the rules.

New Tax Regime vs Old Tax Regime

From FY 2023-24, the new tax regime is the default. Under the new regime, you get lower slab rates but lose most exemptions (HRA, 80C, LTA etc.). Under the old regime, you keep all exemptions but pay higher rates.

For most people earning under ₹10 LPA with a home loan or significant 80C investments, the old regime usually saves more tax. Do the math or ask your HR/CA before choosing.
